# TUNS Final Strap Shortlist

Prepared for a quiet-luxury, classic-menswear-with-a-modern-twist wardrobe built around stainless steel watches, silver jewelry, and a restrained palette of black, white, cream, navy, blue, and brown.

## How To Read This

- The list is ranked in the order I would buy.
- Within each section, `1` is the first-choice listing.
- If that listing is out of stock, move down the ladder in order.
- In each ten-item ladder, `1` through `5` are the purest versions of the recommendation. `6` through `10` are broader tonal fallbacks that still fit the aesthetic but get a little more niche, dressy, or material-specific.
- Every listing now includes the exact target shade, the photo checks that matter, why it outranks its neighbors, and the specific fallback compromise.
- A few TUNS product URLs use odd legacy spelling like `navi`, `allran`, `swilf`, or `boxcafl`. I kept the exact live links.

**Reading shorthand:** `1-5` means the product photo is closest to the exact shade I want. `6-8` means tonal fallback. `9-10` means specialist fallback that usually skews more dressy, more watch-specific, or more mature than the core recommendation.

## 1. Soft Etaupe Everyday Neutral

This is still the best first move in the entire catalog. Etaupe sits between grey, stone, mushroom, and beige in a way that feels expensive because it is hard to choose well. On stainless steel it reads quiet, precise, and fashion-literate rather than loud.

**Works best on:** tank watches, sector dials, silver-dial dress watches, rectangular watches, refined vintage-inspired pieces.

**Best color target:** Cool mushroom / stone taupe with a light-mid depth, muted saturation, matte-dry finish, and soft or fine texture. Avoid creamy beige, camel warmth, or glossy dress shine.

**Closest Figma anchors:**
- Primary light anchor: [Beige](https://www.figma.com/colors/beige/) `#EDE8D0` - use this as the pale stone side of the target; reject straps that turn more yellow or creamy than this.
- Warmth ceiling: [Sand](https://www.figma.com/colors/sand/) `#CBBD93` - useful as the warmest acceptable edge; if a photo goes richer or more camel than Sand, it is too warm for your lane.
- Depth anchor: [Taupe](https://www.figma.com/colors/taupe/) `#54463A` - this is the dark end-stop, not the exact goal; ideal etaupe should read cooler and lighter than Taupe.

## 2. Deep Dark-Brown Nubuck

This is the warm, tactile lane. It reads sensual and intentional rather than rugged, especially against steel. If you wear navy, cream, black, and brown often, this family will get a lot of use and usually looks better in person than in product photos.

**Works best on:** vintage chronographs, cream-dial watches, smaller divers, field watches, dress-casual pieces.

**Best color target:** Neutral-to-cool espresso or bitter chocolate with deep value, muted saturation, matte-dry finish, and soft nap. Avoid chestnut red, orange tobacco, or waxy pull-up glow.

**Closest Figma anchors:**
- Primary anchor: [Dark Brown](https://www.figma.com/colors/dark-brown/) `#654321` - this is the cleanest espresso-like reference for the family and the best first benchmark.
- Softer alternative: [Coffee](https://www.figma.com/colors/coffee/) `#6F4E37` - use this when the strap photo looks a touch more relaxed and cocoa-toned without going red.
- Warmth ceiling: [Sepia Brown](https://www.figma.com/colors/sepia-brown/) `#704214` - if the photo looks warmer or redder than Sepia Brown, it starts leaving the quiet-luxury zone.

## 3. Graphite Suede / Soft Grey Matte

Grey is one of the strongest colors for stainless steel when you want restraint rather than tradition. Done in suede or nubuck, it feels modern, slightly severe, and very coherent with silver jewelry.

**Works best on:** black-dial steel watches, monochrome everyday pieces, modern minimalist watches, cool-toned dress watches.

**Best color target:** Cool slate to charcoal with mid-deep depth, low saturation, matte finish, and soft or fine texture. Avoid taupe warmth, blue cast, or metallic silver glare.

**Closest Figma anchors:**
- Primary anchor: [Gunmetal Gray](https://www.figma.com/colors/gunmetal-gray/) `#353E43` - the strongest benchmark for smoky graphite suede: deep, cool, and urban without becoming black.
- Softer dark edge: [Charcoal](https://www.figma.com/colors/charcoal/) `#4A4A4A` - use this for softer, slightly dustier dark grey photos that still feel grounded and matte.
- Lighter cool edge: [Slate Gray](https://www.figma.com/colors/slate-gray/) `#6D8196` - this is the lighter limit; ideal straps should not get brighter or bluer than this.

## 4. Ink Navy Refined Texture
